Mexico City

What can you see in Mexico City?
  • Coyoacán.
    • Coyoacán is a charming and traditional district in Mexico City, famous for its bohemian atmosphere and rich cultural history. Its name means "place of the coyotes" in Nahuatl, and it is an area that retains many colonial characteristics, with cobblestone streets, squares and vibrantly colored houses.
  • Basílica de Guadalupe.
    • The Basilica de Guadalupe is an important Catholic sanctuary located in Mexico City. It is known for being one of the most visited pilgrimage sites in the world, dedicated to the Virgin of Guadalupe, the patron saint of Mexico and the Americas.
  • Lago de Xochimilco.
    • Lage Xochimilco is one of the most emblematic natural wonders of Mexico City. This system of canals and chinampas (floating islands) is a vestige of the ancient Mexica civilization and has been declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO.
  • Estadio Azteca.
    • The Estadio Azteca is one of the most iconic and recognized stadiums in the world, located in Mexico City. Opened in 1966, it has hosted major sporting events, including two FIFA World Cups in 1970 and 1986, making it the only stadium to have hosted this tournament twice.
  • Teotihuacán.
    • Teotihuacán is one of the most important and fascinating archaeological sites in Mexico, located about 50 kilometers northeast of Mexico City. Its name means "place where the gods were created" in Nahuatl. The place is famous for its impressive pyramids, the most notable being the Pyramid of the Sun and the Pyramid of the Moon.
